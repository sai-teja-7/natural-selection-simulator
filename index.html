Energy Economics

📖 Evolutionary Concepts

Natural Selection: Blobs that survive pass on their traits. Blobs with bad traits die out.

Directional Selection: When an extreme trait (like being super fast) is the best way to survive, the whole group slowly becomes super fast over time.

Carrying Capacity: The board only has enough food for a certain number of blobs. When there are too many, the extra ones starve.

Predator/Prey Arms Race: Meat-eaters must be bigger than their targets. This makes the hunted blobs run faster to get away, which forces the meat-eaters to grow even bigger to catch them!

🧮 Math & Formulae

Speed Tax (Movement Cost)
Moving fast requires huge amounts of energy. The faster they go, the exponentially harder it is.
EnergyLoss = (Size³) × (Speed²) × 1.5 × Tax
Size Tax (Metabolic Cost)
Big blobs burn energy just by existing. Giant predators must hunt constantly.
EnergyLoss = (Size²) × 0.2 × Tax
Sense Tax (Neural Cost)
Seeing further costs a constant drip of energy.
EnergyLoss = VisionRadius × 0.1
